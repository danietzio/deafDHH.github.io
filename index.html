Study Title

Evaluating the impact of visual sound rhythms and emotion-coded captions in online meetings for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing users.

Purpose

This study examines a digital accessibility tool designed to help Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing (DHH) users better understand the emotional tone of speech during online meetings. We compare standard text captions against a new interface that uses color-coded text and visual sound frequency graphs.

What You Will Do

  • Watch short video clips of a person speaking one sentence at a time.
  • After each sentence, select the emotion you perceived.
  • Complete a short NASA Task Load Index (NASA-TLX) questionnaire after each block.
  • Answer a few brief debrief questions at the end.

The entire session takes approximately 15–18 minutes.

Risks & Benefits

Risks are minimal. You may experience mild visual fatigue from watching a screen. Your participation contributes to improving accessible communication tools for the DHH community.

Confidentiality

All data is pseudo-anonymised. No names are stored alongside identifiable information beyond what you enter as your participant ID. Data is held securely and deleted at the end of the project.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without penalty by closing this browser window.
